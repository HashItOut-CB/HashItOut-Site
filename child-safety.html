Child Safety Standards

Our commitment to protecting children from abuse and exploitation

Hash it Out is committed to maintaining a safe platform for all users and takes the protection of children extremely seriously. We have zero tolerance for Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) content and maintain comprehensive policies and systems to prevent, detect, and report such material.

1. Zero Tolerance Policy

Hash it Out has a strict zero tolerance policy for:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Any visual depiction of sexually explicit conduct involving minors
  • Child Sexual Exploitation: Any content that sexualizes, grooms, or endangers children
  • Child Grooming: Attempts to build relationships with minors for the purpose of sexual abuse
  • Child Trafficking: Content facilitating or promoting child trafficking
  • Child Endangerment: Content that poses risks to child safety or wellbeing

Any violation of this policy results in immediate account termination, content removal, and mandatory reporting to the National Centre for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and appropriate law enforcement agencies.

2. Age Restrictions and Verification

2.1 Minimum Age Requirement

Users must be at least 13 years of age to create an account and use Hash it Out, in compliance with:

  • Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) in the United States
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) provisions for minors in the EU/UK
  • Age-appropriate design codes in applicable jurisdictions

2.2 Age Verification

During account creation, users must confirm they meet the minimum age requirement. We employ various technical measures to verify age claims and detect potential violations.

2.3 Parental Controls

For users between 13-17 years of age, we provide additional safety measures including:

  • Enhanced content filtering and moderation
  • Restricted direct communication features
  • Privacy settings that default to more restrictive options
  • Additional monitoring for grooming behavior patterns

3. Content Moderation and Detection

3.1 Proactive Detection Systems

Hash it Out employs multiple layers of protection to detect and prevent CSAE content:

  • Automated Scanning: AI-powered systems scan all user-generated content for potential CSAE material
  • PhotoDNA Technology: We use hash-matching technology to detect known CSAM content
  • Text Analysis: Natural language processing identifies grooming patterns and inappropriate conversations
  • Behavioral Monitoring: Systems detect suspicious patterns of user behavior that may indicate exploitation attempts
  • Image Classification: Machine learning models identify potentially harmful visual content

3.2 Human Review

All flagged content undergoes review by trained content moderation specialists who:

  • Are specifically trained to identify CSAE content and grooming behavior
  • Follow strict protocols for handling and reporting such material
  • Receive ongoing education on emerging threats and tactics
  • Have access to mental health support given the nature of the work

3.3 Prevention Measures

We implement proactive measures to prevent CSAE content from appearing on our platform:

  • Upload restrictions for certain file types commonly associated with CSAE content
  • Keyword filtering to block known grooming language
  • Account creation restrictions for users previously banned for CSAE violations
  • Geofencing to restrict access in high-risk scenarios

4. Reporting Mechanisms

4.1 User Reporting

Hash it Out provides multiple ways for users to report CSAE content or suspicious behavior:

  • In-App Reporting: Report button available on all user-generated content
  • Emergency Reporting: Dedicated channel for urgent child safety concerns at safety@hashitout.net
  • Anonymous Reporting: Users can report content anonymously without fear of retaliation
  • 24/7 Availability: Safety reports are monitored around the clock

4.2 Report Processing

All child safety reports are treated with the highest priority:

  • Immediate review (within 1 hour for emergency reports)
  • Content temporarily removed pending investigation
  • Account suspension for suspected violations
  • Evidence preservation for law enforcement

4.3 External Reporting to Authorities

Hash it Out is legally obligated to report CSAE content and complies with all reporting requirements:

  • NCMEC CyberTipline: All CSAM content found on our platform is reported to the National Centre for Missing & Exploited Children
  • Law Enforcement: We cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations and provide necessary evidence
  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF): UK-based reports are also submitted to the IWF
  • International Cooperation: We work with international agencies through appropriate channels

5. Legal Compliance

5.1 Applicable Laws

Hash it Out complies with all applicable child protection laws including:

  • 18 U.S.C. ยง 2258A (United States CSAM reporting requirements)
  • Protection of Children Act 1978 (United Kingdom)
  • Criminal Justice Act 1988 (United Kingdom)
  • EU Directive 2011/93/EU on combating sexual abuse and exploitation of children
  • All relevant local and international child protection legislation

5.2 Data Retention for Investigations

When CSAE content is detected or reported:

  • Evidence is securely preserved for law enforcement
  • User data related to the violation is retained as required by law
  • Content is removed from public view but maintained for investigative purposes
  • Records are kept in compliance with legal requirements and best practices

6. Partnerships and Collaboration

Hash it Out actively participates in the broader effort to combat online child exploitation through:

  • NCMEC Partnership: Active participant in NCMEC's CyberTipline program
  • Technology Coalition: Member of industry coalitions developing anti-CSAE technologies
  • Law Enforcement Liaison: Designated contacts for law enforcement inquiries
  • Industry Standards: Compliance with best practices from organizations like INHOPE, IWF, and ECPAT
  • Research Collaboration: Support for academic research into CSAE prevention

7. Technology and Innovation

We continuously invest in technology to improve child safety:

  • Machine Learning: Advanced AI models trained to detect emerging CSAE patterns
  • Hash Database: Participation in shared databases of known CSAM content hashes
  • Behavioral Analysis: Systems to identify grooming behavior before harm occurs
  • Age Verification: Ongoing development of more effective age verification methods
  • Regular Audits: Third-party security audits of child safety systems

8. Staff Training and Protocols

All Hash it Out personnel involved in content moderation receive comprehensive training:

  • CSAE identification and classification
  • Grooming tactics and warning signs
  • Legal reporting requirements
  • Trauma-informed practices
  • Evidence preservation procedures
  • Ongoing education on emerging threats

Staff members have access to mental health support and counseling given the potentially traumatic nature of reviewing CSAE content.

9. Response Protocols

9.1 Immediate Actions

When CSAE content is detected or reported, we take immediate action:

  1. Content Removal: Immediate removal of content from the platform (within 1 hour)
  2. Account Suspension: Immediate suspension of the offending account
  3. Evidence Preservation: Secure storage of evidence for law enforcement
  4. Authority Notification: Report to NCMEC, IWF, and/or law enforcement within 24 hours
  5. Related Content Review: Investigation of related accounts and content

9.2 Investigation Process

Our investigation process includes:

  • Review of user's entire content history
  • Analysis of communication patterns
  • Identification of potential victims or additional perpetrators
  • Cross-referencing with known databases of offenders
  • Cooperation with ongoing law enforcement investigations

9.3 Permanent Measures

Following confirmed violations:

  • Permanent account termination with no possibility of reinstatement
  • Ban on creating new accounts (device and email bans)
  • Addition to internal blocklist
  • Continued cooperation with any criminal investigations

10. User Education and Awareness

Hash it Out is committed to educating users about online safety:

  • Safety Resources: In-app resources on recognizing and avoiding grooming
  • Parental Guidance: Materials to help parents discuss online safety with children
  • Warning Signs: Education on identifying suspicious behavior
  • Reporting Information: Clear instructions on how to report concerns
  • Privacy Tips: Best practices for protecting personal information

11. Transparency and Accountability

11.1 Transparency Reports

Hash it Out publishes annual transparency reports including:

  • Number of CSAE reports received
  • Number of accounts terminated for CSAE violations
  • Reports made to NCMEC and other authorities
  • Improvements made to detection systems

11.2 Independent Audits

We undergo regular third-party audits of our child safety practices to ensure compliance with industry best practices and legal requirements.

11.3 Continuous Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving our child safety measures through:

  • Regular review and updating of policies
  • Investment in new detection technologies
  • Participation in industry working groups
  • Response to emerging threats and tactics
  • User feedback incorporation
